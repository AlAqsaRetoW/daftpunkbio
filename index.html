Biography

Daft Punk, a French electronic music duo formed in 1993 by Thomas Bangalter and Guy-Manuel de Homem-Christo, became one of the most influential acts in dance music. Emerging from the French house movement, they blended house music with funk, disco, techno, rock, and synth-pop. After their indie rock band Darlin' disbanded, they released their debut album Homework in 1997, featuring hits like "Around the World" and "Da Funk."
